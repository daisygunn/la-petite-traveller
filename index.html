The go-to guide for any backpacker!

I have been very fortunate to spend a lot of time exploring our beautiful planet. Whilst there are plenty of travel blogs and guides out there, I found that when researching for my first big trip, there just wasn't enough out there about backpacking on a budget that didn't involve missing out on half of the country! I want to give you, my readers, the best tips that I've learnt along the way so that you can make the most of every second & every penny that you have to make life-long memories.

Travel Tips 101

Getting started

You need to have a good idea of where you want to go even if that's just a starting point before contemplating planning (sounds obvious but you'd be surprised how many people just say they want to go away!!).
For my first big backpacking trip, I desperately knew I wanted to start in India and so that was destination 1 - it was only once I was in India that I then started to think about where to go next and made decisions based on flight prices, weather in the surrounding countries and what the food was meant to be like! I'm a vegetarian and so food is always a big factor for me.
This is not the ideal way for many to travel as it does come with some risks but I found not being tied down to flights and routes meant I was able to travel based upon the people I was meeting & recommendations I was being given.

Route planning

Read, read & read again, you are not the first person visiting this country and so there will be plenty of recommendations available for you.
If you're stretched for time then be realistic, moving every day is bound to knacker you out and won't always allow for the most fun to be had! Of course, we are all different people but I find spending 2-3 days in any one place my preferred pace of travel.
Be flexible, have an idea of where you want to go but be open to it changing. Some of my favourite places have been off my planned route - so please do not think you have to stick to any plans!!!

Packing

Pack light - It is KEY to not overpack, get yourself a decent bag (look into how many litres it is) and be sensible. Do you need 6 pairs of shoes? Probably not. You will be so thankful once you're away that your bag doesn't weigh 25kg and that you're able to access all of your items with ease.
It is also very important to consider the countries that you're visiting and ensure that you take appropriate clothing so as not to offend.
Rolling your clothes is the only way to do it, get yourself some packing cubes and organise your stuff into sections, this will make accessing your clothes super quick and easy!
You can find my downloable essentials packing list here.

Budgeting

Be strict! Have a good understanding of how much you have to spend per day/per week/per month and only allow yourself access to this budget.
Research how much travelling from place to place is going to cost, consider all options. Travelling overnight is often the cheapest way to get around as you also save on a night of accommodation!
Depending on which country you visit the hostels may have kitchens, this is a great way to save on money as you can prepare lunch or dinner.
Book somewhere that has breakfast included - makes all the difference getting 1 meal a day for free! Don't be scared of street food, this is often how the locals eat, the food is cheaper and it's normally DELICIOUS.

Toiletries

Shampoo and conditioner bars are a great way to save on space, weight and money. I bought this shampoo and this conditioner and both lasted me my entire 6 months of travelling!!
Always have Imodium & rehydration sachets (or something similar) just in case you get an upset stomach. Having these on hand got me out of a couple of tricky situations.
I also always take a first aid kit with me, loaded with plasters and painkillers - being prepared for anything will make your life so much easier!

Useful apps/websites

Look into the available travel apps for the country you're visiting, some of my faves are:.

  • Busbud is great for buses in North America, South America, Europe, Africa, and Southeast Asia.
  • HostelBookers (use my code LAPETITET123 for an introductory 15% discount)
  • Rome2Rio is great for almost any country you're in, perfect place to start if you're stuck on how to get from A to B.

Packing Essentials

Shoes

Health & Wellness

  • Silk sleeping bag & mosquito net
  • Microfibre travel towel
  • Toiletries
  • First aid kit
  • Mosquito spray (100% deet)

Extras

  • Ear plugs - an absolute necessity for sleeping on trains/in hostels

  • Travel journal - great way to reflect on your time

My Last Trip

Sri Lanka - La Petite Review

Total Budget

£1200

Food Score (out of 10)

A very solid 10/10 - I could not fault it, the curries are delcious, the hoppers, the fruit!!!!!

Internal Travel

As Sri Lanka is relatively small it was super easy to travel, the local buses are somewhat terrifying but a real experience and oh-so cheap.

Best Moment

Safari at Udawalwe - my elephant dreams came true! Also sitting on any of the pictureqesque beaches in the South. Seeing fireflies and a shooting star in Sigiriya!

Low Moment

Genuinely - none.

When to go?

The weather in Sri Lanka is a little crazy for some a small island, the north and south are often very different. I went in the month of December which was fantastic in the South for weather.

Ratings (out of 10)

10 - Sri Lanka has really taken one of my top spots!

My Route

I've made it pretty obvious by now that I fell deeply in love with Sri Lanka, the combination of hikes, nature, food, culture and of course the beaches was something I hadn't expected - it is a real delight and somewhere I would recommend everyone to visit. In fact, I don't think I've shut up about it since I got home. There is something for everyone and being a pretty small country - the UK is about 3.7 times bigger than Sri Lanka - it is quick and easy to get around so perfect for shorter trips too! Below is a map of my route, I had just over 3 weeks in total and as I was travelling over Christmas I had to factor in that everything would be busy and fully booked, after some research I decided not to risk being stranded on Christmas Day and I pre-booked a few places and so had to stick to my schedule. I was also adamant to spend at least 10 days lying on a beach so wanted to move pretty quickly through the beginning of my itinerary.


Negombo - Colombo - Sigirya - Dambulla - Kandy - Ella - Udawalwe - Tallala - Mirissa - Unawatuna - Colombo

Negombo - 1 night
Stayed:
Places to eat:

The main airport in Sri Lanka is called 'Bandaranaike International Colombo Airport' but is actually located closer to Negombo. My flight got in pretty late so I decided to spend my first night and day in Negombo, it was nice to see the beach but there's not much to see here - can easily be missed.

Colombo - 1 night
Stayed:
Places to eat:
Colombo is Sri Lanka's capital and it is a typically big, busy Asian city but has an interesting architectural twist. Due to the colonial rule in the 16th Century, there are a plethora of beautiful colonial buildings mixed in with swanky new office blocks - making this city a real delight to explore. There is an abundance of street food available in the markets and the Pettah Bazaar is definitely worth a visit. The Barefoot bar is an iconic Colombo institution and offers live Jazz on Sundays - this is not a cheap place and so not ideal for those of us on a tight budget! If I'm being totally honest, we probably should have spent more time properly exploring Colombo but with just under a month (and a desperate want to sit on the beach) I wanted to get moving ASAP and so only spent a night.

Sigirya - 2 nights
Stayed:
Places to eat:
As luck would have it there was a train strike when we were in Sri Lanka which meant I had to revert to travelling by bus - we got a bus to Dambulla and then a tuktuk to our guest house. We stayed at Landun Sigirya Villas and I cannot recommend it enough - our host was delightful and made some of the nicest food I've ever eaten in my life. Sigirya is famous for 'Lions Rock' which is an impressive sight from below and offers stunning views from the top. It takes just over an hour to climb and is mostly up stairs. Get there early, avoid the crowds and also the height of the sun! I was also advised by a friend to climb the smaller rock opposite Pidarangula, this is less of a tourist attraction and is more of a wild climb - there are no stairs and it involves some basic climbing. The top offers incredible views of Lions Rock and the climb was a little more fun.

Dambulla - 1 afternoon
We stopped in Dambulla on the way to the bus station as we wanted to visit the Dambulla Cave Temple; it is the largest and best-preserved cave temple complex in Sri Lanka. The rock towers 160 m over the surrounding plains. Major attractions are spread over five caves, which contain statues and paintings. It comes with a small cost of 1500 LUK and is well worth it!

Kandy - 2 nights
Stayed:
Places to eat:
We got the bus to Kandy, the city's heart is scenic Kandy Lake (Bogambara Lake), which is popular for strolling. Kandy is famed for sacred Buddhist sites, including the Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa) shrine, celebrated with the grand Esala Perahera annual procession. We hired a tuk tuk for the day to take us around, we visited the Pinnawella elephant sanctuary (where you can bottle feed baby elephants!!!!) & the Royal Botanical Gardens which are an absolute delight, think Kew Gardens but more exotic with monkeys roaming around! In the evening we visited a traditional Kandyian dance show - you will see adverts everywhere for this - I don't know if it was just the one we visited but it felt like a massive tourist trap so I can't say I would recommend.

Ella - 2 night
Stayed:
Places to eat:
Getting to Ella from Kandy is one of the nicest train rides you'll ever experience and something you absolutley will have seen photos of! YOU MUST BOOK! If you pre-plan far enough in advance (and have the budget) book the observation car as this will offer you panoramic views of the scenery. If, like me, you're unable to book a seat (Christmas tourists meant they sold out) you absolutely will want to get to the train station with ample time to ensure you are by the doors when the train arrives - we were lucky, we got a seat by the window otherwise you'll find yourself sat on the floor with no air-con or breeze! Ella is another real treat for the hikers out there; we arrived relatively late on the first night and so we just had dinner in our guesthouse and hit the sack. The following morning we got up at the crack of dawn to climb Ella's rock - be warned the route to the top is not the easiest and there will be locals that want to guide you (for a fee of course) and may even throw you off the right path to do so! This is a great step-by-step guide to follow. Also in Ella is Adam's Peak, another beautiful trek that is best made in the early hours so that you get to the top for sunrise - we were very unlucky in that our next morning had rain and so we didn't actually go. We did, however, take a walk to the 9 Arch Bridge which provides another iconic view and a great photo op!

Udawalwe - 2 nights
Stayed:
Places to eat:
We decided to do a safari at Udawalawe National Park due to the amount of elephants that live there, we got a bus from Ella and booked our safari through the guest house we were staying at. They picked us up at 5.30am and took us straight to the National Park. WHAT AN EXPERIENCE. We saw an unbelievable amount of animals, elephants - even a baby, crocodiles, bison, peacocks & tropical birds - and the best part is the entrance fee was only £25. A slightly steep activity when living on budget of £30 a day but well worth it. How many times in your life are you going to expeirence that?!

Tallala
Stayed:
Places to eat:
We could not wait until we got to the coast and we were not disappointed; I can honestly say the beaches rival those of the Philippines and in some cases were even nicer, mainly due to the lack of people on them!!! We opted to travel first to Tallala and base ourselves there so that we could explore some of the surrounding beaches. Hiriketiya is just beautiful and great place to surf! Just down from the beach is where I ate one of the best meals of my life. We then visited Dikwella the next day which was completely empty and stunning.

Mirissa
Stayed:
Places to eat:
We got to Mirissa on Christmas eve and stayed in a pretty nice air bnb - it was a real Christmas treat. We partied on the beach and spent christmas day super hungover round our pool and so didn't really explore much! We then visited Wijaya beach where you can snorkel and swim with turtles, rental of the snorkel equipment is cheap. The beach bar here is also delightful!

Unawatuna
Stayed:
Places to eat:
We then moved along to Unawatuna, one of the busier coastal towns and for good reason, there is so much to do and see! It was NYE over our time here and so again didn't do too much exploring, just visited beaches in the day and partied at night! Jungle Beach was really cool and very chill, a great place to snorkel!

Colombo
Stayed:
Places to eat:
Then it was back to Colombo to catch our flight!
